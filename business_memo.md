# Business Memo: Pre-Campaign Retention Strategy Investigation

**To:** Leadership Team

**From:** Data Analytics Department

**Date:** October 26, 2023

**Subject:** Key Areas for Investigation Prior to Launching Customer Retention Campaigns

---

### Executive Summary

Our recent exploratory data analysis (EDA) has illuminated critical insights into customer churn behavior, indicating a substantial churn rate (approximately 47% of our customer base). Before we deploy any new retention campaigns, it is crucial to conduct further, targeted investigations to ensure our efforts are both effective and efficient. This memo outlines the primary areas that warrant deeper scrutiny.

### Key Areas for Investigation

Based on our churn-risk hypotheses, the following areas demand immediate attention:

1.  **Web/App Engagement Deep Dive:**
    *   **Observation:** Customers with consistently fewer web/app engagement (sessions, product views, cart adds) are significantly more likely to churn.
    *   **Investigation Required:** We need to understand *why* engagement drops. Is it a usability issue? Lack of new features? Content relevance? Conduct user surveys, A/B tests on new features, and analyze user flows for friction points. Identify the precise 'moment of disengagement' for churned customers.

2.  **Age Group Specific Churn Drivers:**
    *   **Observation:** Our analysis suggests varying churn rates across different age groups.
    *   **Investigation Required:** Delve deeper into the specific needs and pain points of the age groups exhibiting higher churn. Are their expectations not being met? Are our product offerings or marketing messages less resonant with them? Focus groups or targeted feedback sessions could provide valuable qualitative data.

3.  **Acquisition Channel Performance vs. Retention:**
    *   **Observation:** The acquisition channel appears to influence churn risk.
    *   **Investigation Required:** Analyze the customer lifetime value (CLTV) and 90-day retention rates specifically by acquisition channel. Some channels might be bringing in customers with lower intent or a poor fit for our services. This insight will guide future marketing spend optimization, ensuring we acquire customers who are more likely to stay.

4.  **Impact of Lower Total Spending:**
    *   **Observation:** Customers with lower total spending are more prone to churn.
    *   **Investigation Required:** Determine if lower spending is a cause or a symptom of disengagement. Can we identify specific product categories that fail to drive repeat purchases? Are these customers experiencing issues that deter further spending? Consider micro-segmentation of low-spending customers to understand their journey and potential barriers.

5.  **Root Cause Analysis for Critical Support Issues:**
    *   **Observation:** Certain types of support tickets (e.g., 'technical_issue', 'billing_issue') are associated with higher churn rates.
    *   **Investigation Required:** Conduct a thorough root cause analysis for these high-churn-risk issue types. Is there a systemic product flaw, a gap in our documentation, or an inconsistency in our support resolution process? Improving the resolution of these specific issues could have a significant impact on retention.

### Recommendation

By proactively investigating these areas, we can move beyond correlation to causation. This data-driven approach will enable us to design highly targeted, impactful retention campaigns that address the true underlying reasons for churn, rather than simply treating symptoms. This will maximize the return on investment for our retention efforts and foster a more loyal customer base.
